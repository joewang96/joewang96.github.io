A summary of the Boo!Boston project

For my Interaction Design 2 course at Northeastern, I worked in a team on a semester long project aimed at creating an app concept for a small design firm. The app idea was an augmented reality (AR) app titled Boo!Boston, which would in theory run from May through August in Boston and help drive tourism into the city. Our task was to created a possible visual direction the app could go in, as well as create user flows that would help keep the user engaged and interested in the app. Gameplay was not a focus of the project, but designing interactions was key.

Throughout the semester my team worked on creating a service concept that our app would provide in order to help gain traction and support from the city and local buisnesses. We created a user journey map in order to gain insights into how the interactions would happen over the course of using the app, and created the overlaying architecture for the screens and how each would link with the rest.

The final concept for Boo!Boston

Our final concept surrounded the use of a friendly guide to take users from high traffic areas to local hidden gems and small buisnesses. The initial ghost we were given to model the app around was home to the Fenway neighborhood, and we felt that with the wide variety of surrounding bars and local resturants would be a perfect fit for this kind of service concept. Additionally, the neighborhood is typically crowded, making the need to find a less populated path higher. Local buisnesses would see an increase in foot-traffic, resulting in higher overall sales.
